At some point during the pandemic, Zoom stopped being a brand name and became a verb. Few companies in history have achieved that. The ones that do will tell you it is a more complicated gift than it appears. Because when the category shifts, the verb shifts with it.  
  
Sameer Raje, Country Leader and Head of India and SAARC at Zoom, joins Front Page to explain how Zoom is repositioning from pandemic hero to an AI-first enterprise platform. He breaks down the philosophy of "conversation to completion" (prep before the meeting, assist during, create tasks after), why Zoom Ai Companion orchestrates workflows across multiple agents and departments to break down organizational silos, why the company embeds both proprietary and industry standard LLMs (OpenAI, Anthropic) rather than going "our way or the highway," and how Zoom Phone (cloud-based PBX) and Zoom Contact Center (omnichannel CX platform) have evolved the company beyond just meetings.  
  
He also covers India as Zoom's second-largest market and why the talent pool is critical to global R&D, how Zoom integrates with Microsoft 365, Google Workspace and legacy systems rather than forcing replacement, 256-bit GCM encryption and bring-your-own-key architecture for data security, and why human-to-human connection will remain the core even as collaboration formats evolve with digital twins and new technologies.
