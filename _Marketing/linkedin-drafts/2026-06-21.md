## 1. CONTRARIAN — We stopped selling software to hospitals. We now sell automation power to employees.

```
Everyone sells software to hospitals. We stopped.

We built a 100-bed hospital HIS, charged ₹2–3 lakhs, struggled for 18 months watching it sit in a drawer. IT teams didn't own it. Workflows didn't change. ROI was invisible.

So we asked a billing clerk what she actually needed.

Not a system. Not a dashboard. Automation power. A markdown file with her SOP, a tiny Agent Factory UI, and permission to map her own daily friction (approval chains, duplicate entry, manual reconciliation) into a bot that runs at 5am.

She owned it. She iterated it. Within 3 weeks, she cut her reconciliation time by 4 hours a week.

Now every employee is an automation builder. We sell the platform, not the solution. Hospitals get 20+ automations running bottom-up instead of one massive system gathering dust.

Everyone's still selling software. We're selling the power to automate your own chaos.
```

## 2. CONTRARIAN — Scope creep reads as ambition. It's usually just math you haven't done.

```
Scope creep reads as ambition. It's usually just math you haven't done.

We pitched an AI integration for HR. The scope expanded immediately—payroll module, compliance tagging, cross-tenant data. Bigger meant better, obviously.

We ran a pilot to measure the real friction: integration complexity, data cleanup, approval overhead. Six approval gates per data type instead of one.

Take that pilot friction and scale it across five departments. You hit a $200K leak in invisible rework.

So we locked the scope: HR only. Three agents. Model choice TBD post-pilot.

Finance wanted to wait. Operations said they'd prioritize it later.

Deal closed without pushback.

Scope creep isn't ambition. It's just cost you haven't calculated yet.
```

## 3. FRAMEWORK — 24-7-30: Our framework for doctor relationship building—24h contact, 7d engagement, 30d follow-up.

```
24-7-30. That's our doctor relationship framework now.

24 hours to initial contact after a referral. Not "we'll get back to them eventually." You call. Today.

7 days of active social media engagement—comments on their posts, not bot likes. Signals you're actually paying attention.

30 days to an in-person meeting with A1 doctors. Coffee, site visit, doesn't matter. Real presence in their world.

The friction was obvious once we stopped ignoring it: doctors send us patients, then we ghost them. They don't know if we care about their cases or if they're just numbers. Referrals dry up.

This rule kills the ambiguity. No interpretation, no "let's see how it goes." You hit the dates or you don't.

Early read: A1 doctors we've engaged with are sending 3x their previous volume. Rules beat hope every time.
```
