## 1. FRAMEWORK — We froze all project deliverables in a PDF before day one—kills scope creep at the source.

```
**Freeze scope in a PDF before day one.**

You can't renegotiate what's in writing. Every scope creep starts soft—a Slack message, a call, "just one more thing." By the time you've negotiated three times, you've lost your timeline.

Here's what we do: requirements go into a deliverables PDF before any dev work starts. Not a Doc. Not a spreadsheet. A PDF. It doesn't change mid-project.

On Decode My Brain (WordPress to Laravel migration), the old platform was so broken we ended up rebuilding half of it. But because the new scope was locked in a PDF on day one, when the client asked for three extra features mid-sprint, we had one document to point to. "This is what we agreed. These are change orders."

One variable changed: one frozen PDF instead of seven rolling conversations. Result: timeline held, client didn't feel blindsided.

The PDF isn't a contract. It's a referee.
```

## 2. CONTRARIAN — We deleted the free tier. Stripe payment upfront, no exceptions—here's what happened to conversion.

```
Everyone says free tier builds your moat. We deleted ours.

Stripe payment upfront. No trial. No freemium. No "explore for 14 days" button. You want automated assessments? You pay first.

Signups dropped 40% in week one. Our support inbox actually cleared.

But here's what happened: users who converted became real customers. Schools and corporate L&D teams didn't ask "is this free?" They asked "how many students per batch?" and "does this integrate with our HRMS?" The tire-kickers evaporated. The builders stayed.

Corporate contracts went from 3-4 per quarter to 12+. ARR grew 280%.

And the feedback shifted—paying customers care deeply about whether you ship. Free users complain about button colors.

Free tiers are excellent for vanity metrics. We wanted revenue instead.
```
