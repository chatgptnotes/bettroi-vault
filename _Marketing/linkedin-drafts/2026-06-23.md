## 1. FRAMEWORK — We consolidated scattered Slack channels and meeting notes into one 'Pulse of Project' source-of-truth. Here's the template.

```
**The Pulse of Project rule: one document becomes your project's heartbeat. Everything else is noise.**

Slack channels fragment fast. A video link dies when someone leaves. Notes exist in five different places. Three months later, nobody remembers why you picked this vendor or why that feature got killed.

We started dumping project state into one weekly "Pulse" doc — blockers, decisions, next moves. Recordings go into Drive (survive Slack, searchable, persistent). Single source of truth. Team leads own it.

Meeting prep time dropped 60%. We stopped rehashing decisions from month-old threads.

Catch: it only works if your leads actually fill it in. Not another graveyard doc.
```

## 2. CONTRARIAN — We killed the free tier. Paid-only via Stripe Cashier is forcing better customer fit and unit economics.

```
Everyone says free tiers drive SaaS adoption. We killed ours.

Stripe Cashier, paid-only from day one. No freemium, no trial, no "upgrade later" button.

Customer fit improved immediately. People who commit budget show up and stay. We see usage in real time. Churn signal is instant—payment fails, engagement drops, we know it's coming.

The free tier wasn't driving adoption. It was drowning us in tire-kickers. Support overhead down. Refund disputes down. Quality of customers up.

Unit economics flipped when we stopped pretending skeptics would eventually convert.

If they won't pay upfront, they won't pay at all.
```

## 3. ANECDOTE — Form routing collapsed across 10+ workflows. One JotForm CC setting fixed it—saving 5 hours/month in manual distribution.

```
Our form routing was a mess. Ten-plus workflows, each spitting submissions into different inboxes, different team members missing notifications, vendors never getting their pre-filled links.

The bottleneck: everyone was manually copying form URLs and distributing them via email. Every. Single. One.

Then someone noticed JotForm had a CC field we'd never used.

Added one email address to the CC settings across all workflows. Submissions now land in a central inbox. The app reads them, extracts the pre-filled link, surfaces it to the user directly. No human in the loop.

Five hours a month just... gone.

Fourteen thousand existing form links are still in the old system. We're still debating whether to migrate those. But every new workflow gets the CC fix automatically now.

The lesson: The tool has the feature. You're just not using it.
```
