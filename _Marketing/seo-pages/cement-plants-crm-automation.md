# CRM Automation for Cement Plants

## The Problem

Cement plant sales teams manage hundreds of dealers, contractors, and institutional buyers across regions — but most of this is tracked in Excel files that no one updates on time. RFQ responses go out late, contract renewals get missed by weeks, and your sales managers spend 3–4 hours a day chasing status updates instead of closing. The CRM you bought sits at 20% adoption because nobody entered the data consistently in the first place.

## What We Build

- **Dealer pipeline agent** — auto-captures enquiry data from WhatsApp, email, and calls; logs it to your CRM without manual entry
- **RFQ follow-up bot** — tracks open quotations and fires contextual follow-up messages at day 2, day 5, and day 10 with no human intervention
- **Contract renewal alert agent** — flags expiring dealer and institutional contracts 45 days out, drafts renewal communication, assigns to the right RM
- **Sales call summariser** — transcribes and summarises field rep calls, extracts next actions, pushes them into CRM tasks automatically
- **Regional performance dashboard agent** — aggregates pipeline data across depots and regions, generates a weekly summary report for the sales head
- **Lead scoring model** — ranks inbound dealer enquiries by order size potential, geography fit, and historical conversion rate

## How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We don't drop software on your team and disappear. Every engagement runs through five stages:

**1. Discovery (Week 1–2)**
We audit your current CRM state, sales workflow, and data quality. We identify the 3 highest-ROI automation candidates specific to your plant's sales structure — whether that's a 2-depot regional setup or a 15-state dealer network.

**2. Learn (Weeks 3–8)**
We pilot one agent — usually the RFQ follow-up bot or the dealer pipeline agent — on a live workflow. We measure baseline response time, follow-up rate, and pipeline conversion against the AI-assisted version over 30 days. Numbers on the table before we go further.

**3. Wire (Weeks 6–10)**
Winning agents get integrated into your existing CRM (Salesforce, Zoho, HubSpot, or custom). We connect dealer portals, email, WhatsApp Business API, and ERP data feeds where needed.

**4. Automate (Weeks 8–12)**
Manual handoffs — RFQ logging, follow-up scheduling, renewal tracking — get replaced by agent orchestration. Your team sees tasks and summaries, not raw data entry.

**5. Scale**
We roll out across depots and regions with usage monitoring, alert governance, and quarterly tuning. Agents get smarter as more deal data flows through.

## Frequently asked questions

**Our dealers use WhatsApp, not email. Can your agents work with WhatsApp Business?**

Yes. We integrate with the WhatsApp Business API to capture enquiry data, send follow-ups, and log conversations directly into your CRM. We've handled this for other sales workflows where email adoption was low.

**We already have a CRM — Zoho or Salesforce. Do we need to replace it?**

No. We build agents on top of your existing CRM using its API layer. The goal is to drive adoption and automate data entry — not to rip and replace software you've already paid for.

**Our regional sales team is not very tech-savvy. Will they actually use this?**

That's the right concern to raise. Our agents are designed so the field rep experiences almost no change — they keep using WhatsApp and phone calls. The agent captures and logs in the background. Managers see the structured pipeline in the CRM. Adoption isn't a training problem when the reps don't have to change their behaviour.

**How long before we see actual results — not just a demo?**

The Learn stage runs for 30 days on a live workflow with baseline metrics tracked from day one. By week 8 of a Pilot engagement, you have real numbers: follow-up response rate before vs. after, pipeline data completeness, and RM time saved per week.

**Is our sales data safe? We deal with institutional buyers and government contracts.**

All data is processed in your own cloud environment or a dedicated tenant — we do not use shared infrastructure for client data. We can work within your existing cloud vendor (AWS, Azure, GCP) and sign an NDA and data processing agreement before the Discovery audit begins.
