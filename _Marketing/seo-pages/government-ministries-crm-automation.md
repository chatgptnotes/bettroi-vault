# CRM Automation for Government Ministries


## Government CRM runs on spreadsheets, shared inboxes, and institutional memory — and it shows.

Most ministry CRM setups were designed to log interactions, not resolve them. Citizen queries get assigned, reassigned, escalated, and then quietly stall. Service officers spend 40–60% of their day on follow-up calls and status emails that an agent could handle in seconds. Meanwhile, audit trails are incomplete, SLA compliance is untracked, and senior leadership has no live view of service delivery performance across departments or regions.

---

## What We Build

- **Citizen query intake agent** — classifies inbound requests by department, urgency, and SLA tier automatically at submission
- **AI-driven case routing engine** — assigns cases to the right officer/desk based on workload, specialisation, and past resolution data
- **Automated follow-up and status notification bot** — sends citizens proactive updates at defined workflow milestones, no officer action required
- **Escalation trigger agent** — flags cases breaching SLA thresholds and routes them to supervisors with full context attached
- **Ministry-wide CRM dashboard** — live view of open cases, resolution rates, average handling time, and SLA compliance by department
- **Document and correspondence automation** — drafts acknowledgement letters, closure notices, and inter-department memos from structured case data

## How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We do not sell software licences and leave you to figure out adoption. We run a structured 5-stage engagement that ends with agents actually operating inside your ministry's workflows.

**1. Discovery (2 weeks)**
We audit your current CRM stack, intake channels, escalation paths, and officer workload. We identify the 3 highest-ROI automation candidates — typically: intake triage, follow-up notifications, and SLA escalation.

**2. Learn (weeks 3–6)**
We deploy a pilot agent on one workflow — usually the highest-volume intake queue — and run it in parallel with the existing process for 30 days. We measure baseline handling time, officer effort, and citizen response rates against the AI-assisted numbers.

**3. Wire (weeks 7–9)**
Winning agents get integrated into your live CRM — whether that is Salesforce Government Cloud, ServiceNow, Microsoft Dynamics, or a custom-built system. We handle the API connections, data mapping, and access controls.

**4. Automate (weeks 10–11)**
Manual handoffs between intake, assignment, follow-up, and closure get replaced with agent orchestration. Officers handle exceptions and complex cases. Routine workflows run without intervention.

**5. Scale (week 12 and beyond)**
We roll out across additional departments or regional offices with monitoring dashboards, escalation governance, and a monthly review cadence built in.

## Frequently asked questions

**Our ministry already has a CRM system. Do you replace it or work with what we have?**

We work with your existing CRM — Salesforce, ServiceNow, Microsoft Dynamics, or custom-built. We add AI agents on top of your current stack via APIs. There is no rip-and-replace. The Discovery audit maps exactly which integrations are required before we commit to any build.

**How do you handle data sensitivity and government data residency requirements?**

All agents are architected to run within your existing data perimeter — on-premise, private cloud, or government-approved cloud regions. We do not route citizen data through third-party SaaS without explicit clearance. Data handling protocols are agreed during Discovery and documented before any pilot goes live.

**Our officers are resistant to automation. How do you manage change?**

The Learn stage is specifically designed to build internal credibility. Officers see their own baseline numbers compared against AI-assisted numbers after 30 days. When the data is visible, the conversation shifts from 'will this work' to 'where else can we use it'. We also structure pilots so officers handle exceptions — which are more interesting than routine follow-up calls anyway.

**What does the Discovery audit actually deliver?**

A written report covering: current CRM workflow map, volume and handling-time data by case type, identification of the top 3 automation candidates ranked by ROI, a rough implementation roadmap, and an honest assessment of where your data quality will constrain AI performance. It is a decision document, not a sales deck.

**Can you work within government procurement and tendering constraints?**

Yes. We have structured engagements to fit GeM portal procurement, direct empanelment, and limited-tender processes depending on the ministry's rules. The Discovery audit is typically small enough to clear single-source or petty-cash procurement limits in most state and central departments.
