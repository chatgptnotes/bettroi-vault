The Problem

Cement plant operations generate thousands of sensor readings per hour, but most plants are still making kiln corrections reactively — after a quality deviation or a trip, not before. Shift handover reports are written manually, which means the incoming supervisor is working from a 30-minute-old summary that missed the last two alarms. And when equipment does go down, maintenance teams spend hours pulling historical logs that an agent could surface in seconds.

---

What We Build

  • Kiln anomaly detection agent — monitors temperature, pressure, and drive current in real time; flags pre-trip conditions with 15–30 min lead time
  • Raw mix optimization agent — reads lab results and feed bin levels, recommends blend corrections without waiting for a shift chemist
  • Shift handover documentation bot — auto-generates structured handover reports from SCADA/DCS logs at end of each shift
  • Procurement exception agent — monitors PO lifecycle, flags delays on critical spares (lubricants, refractory, grinding media) before they cause unplanned downtime
  • P&ID extraction pipeline — converts engineering drawings into structured equipment and instrumentation data for asset registers and maintenance workflows
  • Compliance reporting agent — compiles emission readings, energy consumption logs, and safety checklists into regulator-ready formats automatically

How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We do not drop software and walk away. Every engagement runs through five stages:

1. Discovery (2 weeks) We audit your current workflows — DCS/SCADA data flows, shift reporting, maintenance ticketing, procurement cycles — and identify the 3 highest-ROI AI candidates specific to your plant. You get a written findings report regardless of whether you continue.

2. Learn (30 days) We pilot one agent on one workflow. Baseline is measured before day one. At day 30, you have hard numbers: time saved, error rate change, lead-time improvement. No pilot runs on faith.

3. Wire Winning agents are integrated into your actual systems — SCADA, SAP PM, your ERP, your historian (OSIsoft PI, Aveva, or equivalent). No parallel systems that die when the consultant leaves.

4. Automate Manual handoffs between shifts, between lab and control room, between maintenance and procurement — replaced with agent orchestration. Humans stay in the loop for decisions; agents handle the data movement and alerting.

5. Scale Roll out across kilns, grinding units, and sister plants with monitoring dashboards and governance guardrails. New sites onboard faster because the agent logic is already proven.

Frequently asked questions

Our plant already has a DCS and historian. Do you need us to replace anything?

No. We integrate with what you have — OSIsoft PI, Aveva, Wonderware, Ignition, or custom historians. The agent layer sits on top of your existing data infrastructure. We do not ask you to rip and replace.

How is this different from the AI features our DCS vendor already offers?

Vendor-bundled AI is usually limited to their own sensor data and their own UI. Our agents cross system boundaries — pulling from SCADA, ERP, lab systems, and maintenance tickets simultaneously — and they can trigger actions across those systems, not just display alerts inside one dashboard.

What does the 30-day pilot actually measure?

We agree on a baseline metric before day one — for example, average time from kiln anomaly to operator response, or hours spent per week on shift handover documentation. At day 30, we measure the same metric with the agent running. You get a one-page results summary with the numbers.

Do we need a data science team internally to manage this after you leave?

No. We build agents for operations teams to run, not for data scientists to babysit. Monitoring dashboards are designed for shift supervisors and plant managers. If something breaks or needs retraining, that is covered under retained operations or a support SLA.

We have three plants across two states. Can the agents work across all sites?

Yes — the Scale stage is specifically designed for multi-site rollout. Agent logic proven at plant one is packaged and deployed to additional sites with configuration changes, not full rebuilds. Each site gets its own monitoring but shares the same governance framework.
