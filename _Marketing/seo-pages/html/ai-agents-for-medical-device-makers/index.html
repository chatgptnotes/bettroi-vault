The Problem

Medical device makers carry a documentation and compliance burden that grows every year — MDR filings, sterilization batch records, implant traceability logs — and most of it is still handled by humans copy-pasting between ERP, QMS, and email. How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We don't drop software and disappear. Every engagement runs through five stages — you decide how far you want to go.

Stage 1 — Discovery (2 weeks)

We audit your current workflows: QMS processes, ERP data flows, complaint handling, regulatory reporting cadence. We identify the 3 highest-ROI AI candidates specific to your device category and manufacturing setup. Fixed-fee, no fluff.

Stage 2 — Learn (30 days)

We build and deploy one agent on one workflow. We measure baseline time-on-task vs. AI-assisted for 30 days. You see a real number — hours saved per week, error rate before and after — before committing to anything bigger.

Stage 3 — Wire

The winning agent gets integrated into your core systems: your QMS (MasterControl, Qualio, or custom), ERP (SAP, Oracle, Tally), CRM, and wherever your sterilization or manufacturing data lives. No manual export-import.

Stage 4 — Automate

Manual handoffs between QA, regulatory affairs, and field service get replaced with agent orchestration. Escalation rules are encoded. Humans stay in the loop where regulations require it; the rest runs on its own.

Stage 5 — Scale

Roll out across product lines, manufacturing sites, or geographies. We add monitoring dashboards, governance guardrails, and access controls so your quality head can sleep at night.

Frequently asked questions

We're a Class B/C device maker under CDSCO. Will your agents produce documentation that's audit-ready?

The agents produce structured draft outputs grounded on your own SOPs and regulatory templates — your QA team reviews and signs off before any record is finalised. We don't bypass your approval chain; we eliminate the blank-page and data-gathering work that precedes it. We've worked with surgical implant documentation workflows and understand what an auditor expects to see.

Our manufacturing data lives in a mix of SAP, a legacy QMS, and Excel sheets. Can you integrate with that?

Yes, this is the normal situation. In the Wire stage we build connectors to wherever your data actually lives — SAP APIs, QMS exports, even structured Excel files. We've integrated agents with hospital management systems and SCADA-adjacent data sources. Messy data environments are the rule, not the exception.

How do you handle the 21 CFR Part 11 / EU MDR requirement for electronic records and signatures?

Agent-generated drafts are flagged as drafts until a qualified human reviewer approves them in your existing QMS. We don't create a parallel records system — we slot into your validated system's workflow. If your QMS supports e-signatures (most do), the approval chain stays fully compliant. We scope the exact compliance architecture during Discovery.

We have a 20-person quality team. Will this replace them?

No. The agents handle the mechanical, repetitive work — pulling data, formatting records, cross-checking lot numbers, drafting first-cut reports. Your quality team shifts from data entry to actual quality judgement. In our experience, a 20-person team with agents working alongside them can handle 2-3x the throughput without headcount increase.

What's the minimum size of company this makes sense for?

If you're filing at least one MDR/vigilance report per quarter and your QA team spends more than 10 hours a week on documentation that doesn't require expert judgment, the Discovery audit will pay for itself. We've seen this apply to manufacturers with as few as 50 employees and annual revenues above ₹5 crore.
