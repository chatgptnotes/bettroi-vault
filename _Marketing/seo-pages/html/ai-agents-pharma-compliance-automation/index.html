The Problem

Pharma compliance teams spend 40–60% of their week on documentation tasks that add zero scientific value: copying batch data into deviation forms, chasing sign-offs across departments, reconciling SOP versions across sites. A single Schedule M or 21 CFR Part 11 audit can demand 200+ documents in under a week, most assembled by hand. When a compliance officer leaves, that institutional knowledge of "where everything lives" leaves with them.

How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We run a structured 5-stage engagement so you're not buying a black box.

1. Discovery (2 weeks) We audit your current compliance workflows — QMS software, document management systems, ERP integrations, manual handoffs. We map exactly where time is lost and identify the 3 highest-ROI automation candidates in your QA/RA function.

2. Learn (weeks 3–6) We deploy a pilot agent on one workflow — typically deviation capture or CAPA tracking — and measure baseline manual effort vs. AI-assisted effort over 30 days. You see real numbers before committing further.

3. Wire (weeks 7–8) Winning agents are integrated into your existing stack: Veeva Vault, MasterControl, SAP QM, or even SharePoint-based QMS setups. No rip-and-replace.

4. Automate (weeks 9–10) Manual handoffs between QA, production, and RA are replaced with agent-orchestrated workflows. Approvals route automatically. Escalations trigger without human intervention.

5. Scale (weeks 11–12) Agents roll out across additional product lines, manufacturing sites, or regulatory markets with governance dashboards and monitoring baked in from day one.

Frequently asked questions

Our QMS is already on Veeva Vault / MasterControl. Do your agents replace it or plug into it?

We plug in, not replace. Our agents sit on top of your existing QMS via API or RPA layer — reading data, triggering workflows, and writing back structured outputs. You keep your validated system of record. No revalidation headache.

How do you handle 21 CFR Part 11 and data integrity requirements?

Every agent action is logged with timestamp, user attribution, and immutable audit trail — the same standard required under 21 CFR Part 11. We design for data integrity from the architecture stage, not as an afterthought. If your QMS already has a validated audit trail, our agents write to it rather than creating a parallel one.

What's the realistic time to first value — when do we stop doing things manually?

In the Learn stage (weeks 3–6), you run one agent in parallel with your existing manual process. By week 6, you have a real 30-day comparison of time spent and error rates. Most clients see the first meaningful manual effort reduction in week 4. Full manual handoff replacement happens in the Automate stage, around week 9–10.

Our compliance team is 4 people and they're skeptical of AI. How do you handle adoption?

We've seen this in every regulated environment we've worked in. The Discovery stage includes a half-day working session with the actual QA team — not just management — to map their workflows. Agents are designed around how they already work, not a theoretical ideal. The pilot runs alongside existing processes so nothing breaks while trust is being built.

Can you work with Indian regulatory requirements — CDSCO, Schedule M, GMP?

Yes. Our team has direct operational exposure to Indian healthcare regulation through NABH-accredited hospital operations. We understand the documentation expectations of CDSCO inspections and Schedule M compliance, and we build submission prep agents calibrated to Indian GMP dossier formats — not just US FDA or EU GMP templates.
