The Problem

Pharma field teams log 20–40 doctor visits a week, but CRM data entry happens at 10 PM on a phone — rushed, incomplete, and useless for forecasting. Managers pull territory reports manually because no one trusts the CRM numbers anyway. Meanwhile, HCP engagement data, sample disbursement records, and CME attendance sit in three different systems that don't talk to each other.

How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We don't start by selling you software. We start by finding out where your CRM workflow is actually bleeding time and money.

Stage 1 — Discovery (2 weeks) We audit your current field force workflow end-to-end: how reps log visits, how managers report, where data drops off. We identify the 3 CRM processes where AI automation will return the clearest ROI — typically visit logging, HCP scoring, and territory reporting.

Stage 2 — Learn (30 days) We deploy a pilot AI agent on one workflow — usually visit auto-logging, because that's where rep frustration is highest and the baseline is easiest to measure. We run it live for 30 days alongside your existing process, measure time saved per rep per day, and data completeness improvement before asking you to commit to anything bigger.

Stage 3 — Wire Winning agents get integrated into your actual CRM stack — Salesforce, Veeva, Zoho, or a custom build. No parallel systems. No CSV exports. The agent lives inside the tools your team already uses.

Stage 4 — Automate Manual handoffs between field rep, area manager, and regional head get replaced with agent orchestration. Escalations, approvals, and performance alerts flow automatically, with full audit trails.

Stage 5 — Scale We roll out across all territories and business units, with monitoring dashboards, usage governance, and retraining protocols as your product portfolio or team structure changes.

Frequently asked questions

Will this work with Veeva CRM, or only with Salesforce?

We've built integrations on top of Veeva, Salesforce, and Zoho. If you're on a custom or legacy CRM, we assess API availability during the Discovery audit before committing to a Wire-stage scope. Most pharma CRMs expose enough to make agent integration feasible without replacing the underlying system.

Our reps are not very tech-savvy. Will they actually use this?

The whole point of the visit auto-logging agent is that reps don't need to change their behaviour much — they speak a voice note or send a WhatsApp message, and the agent structures it into CRM fields. We measure adoption rate as a primary KPI during the 30-day Learn stage. If adoption is below threshold, we iterate the UX before moving to Wire.

How does the 30-day pilot work in practice — do we run it parallel to our existing process?

Yes. During the Learn stage, the agent runs alongside your current process so there's no operational risk. We compare agent-generated CRM entries against manually-entered ones for completeness, accuracy, and time taken. At the end of 30 days you have a measured baseline-vs-AI comparison, not a vendor's slide deck.

Can the HCP engagement scoring agent handle different therapy area segmentation — cardiology vs dermatology reps, for instance?

Yes. Scoring models are parameterised by product group and territory type during the Wire stage. A cardiology rep visiting a cardiologist three times a quarter means something different from the same frequency in a general physician territory. We configure scoring logic to reflect your actual commercial priorities, not a generic template.

What happens if a key agent fails mid-month — who supports it?

Clients on the Full Transformation or Retained AI Ops engagement get a defined SLA for agent uptime and incident response. For pilot and Discovery clients, we provide a direct escalation line. All agents are built with fallback logging so a failure degrades gracefully — reps get notified to log manually — rather than silently dropping data.
