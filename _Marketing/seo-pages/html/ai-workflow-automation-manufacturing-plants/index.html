How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We don't arrive with a generic SaaS demo. We run a structured 5-stage engagement calibrated to your plant's actual systems and workflows.

Stage 1 — Discovery (2 weeks)

We audit your current workflows — shift handovers, maintenance cycles, procurement, quality reporting — and identify the 3 highest-ROI AI candidates ranked by time saved, error rate, and integration complexity. You get a written report whether or not you proceed further.

Stage 2 — Learn (weeks 3–6)

We build and deploy a pilot agent on one workflow. We measure baseline (your current process) vs. AI-assisted for 30 days. Numbers on the table: time per task, error rate, staff hours recovered.

Stage 3 — Wire (weeks 7–9)

Winning agents get integrated into your core systems — SCADA, ERP, CMMS, QMS, or even your existing Excel/WhatsApp stack where that's the reality. No rip-and-replace.

Stage 4 — Automate (weeks 9–11)

Manual handoffs are replaced with agent orchestration. Approvals, escalations, and exception handling run on defined logic. Your team intervenes only where human judgment is genuinely needed.

Stage 5 — Scale (week 12 onwards)

Agents roll out across shifts, lines, and sites with usage monitoring, retraining triggers, and governance controls so nothing drifts silently.

Frequently asked questions

We already have an ERP and SCADA. Do your agents replace them or work alongside them?

They work alongside. We integrate agents into whatever systems you already run — SAP, Oracle, Infor, Wonderware, Ignition, or a homegrown stack. The agent sits on top as an orchestration layer, not a replacement. You don't lose your existing data or licences.

Our plant runs 24/7 across 3 shifts. Can agents handle that without human supervision?

Yes, that's the exact scenario agents are built for. Shift handover agents, maintenance alert bots, and procurement intake agents run continuously. Exceptions — anything outside defined logic — are escalated to a named human. You define the escalation rules during the Wire stage.

How long before we see actual ROI, not just a pilot report?

The Learn stage (weeks 3–6) produces a 30-day baseline vs. AI comparison with real numbers: hours saved per week, reduction in handoff errors, approval turnaround time. Most clients recover the pilot cost within one quarter on a single workflow. Full transformation ROI depends on scope, but we scope conservatively and don't promise outcomes we can't measure.

What if our plant data is messy — inconsistent formats, paper-based records, no clean API?

That's the norm, not the exception. Our P&ID Extractor and document parsing pipeline are specifically built to handle unstructured plant data — scanned drawings, PDFs, Excel logs, even WhatsApp message exports. We handle the data normalisation as part of the Discovery and Wire stages.

Do we need an in-house IT team to maintain this after you hand it over?

No. We offer retained AI Ops on a monthly subscription — agent monitoring, retraining when accuracy drifts, and new workflow rollouts as your needs change. If you do have an internal team, we document everything so they can take over. Most mid-size plants opt for retained ops for at least the first year.
