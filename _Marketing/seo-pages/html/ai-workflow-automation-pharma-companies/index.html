The Problem

Pharma operations run on a maze of disconnected systems — ERP, LIMS, QMS, batch record software — with humans bridging every gap via email threads and Excel trackers. Regulatory submissions get delayed because document collation is still a copy-paste job across three departments. Batch release cycles stretch days longer than they should because deviation review, QA sign-off, and dispatch scheduling have no automated handoff between them.

---

What We Build

Depending on your workflow audit findings, Bettroi typically delivers one or more of the following:

  • Batch documentation agent — auto-generates batch manufacturing records (BMRs) and batch packaging records (BPRs) from LIMS and ERP data, flags anomalies before QA review
  • Regulatory dossier assembly bot — pulls CTD module data from source systems, compiles submission-ready documents, tracks version history
  • Deviation and CAPA workflow agent — routes deviation reports to the right reviewer, tracks CAPA deadlines, sends escalation alerts without human chasing
  • Vendor qualification automation — extracts and scores supplier documents against your approved vendor list criteria, drafts qualification reports
  • Sales-to-supply chain coordination agent — translates confirmed orders into production scheduling inputs, reduces demand-supply mismatch lag
  • SOP and training compliance tracker — monitors training completion against role requirements, flags gaps ahead of audits

How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We do not drop software and disappear. Every engagement follows five stages:

1. Discovery (2 weeks) We audit your current workflows end-to-end — batch release, regulatory, QA, supply chain — and identify the 3 highest-ROI automation candidates with estimated time and cost savings per candidate.

2. Learn (weeks 3–6) We deploy a pilot agent on one workflow. We measure baseline performance for the first two weeks, then run the AI agent in parallel for 30 days. You see exact before/after numbers — cycle time, error rate, headcount hours — before committing further.

3. Wire (weeks 7–9) Winning agents get integrated into your actual systems: SAP, Oracle, Veeva, or whatever stack you run. No rip-and-replace. We build on top of what you already have.

4. Automate (weeks 9–11) Manual handoffs between departments get replaced with agent orchestration. Humans stay in the loop for decisions; agents handle the routing, drafting, tracking, and alerting.

5. Scale (week 12 onward) We roll the playbook across sites or additional workflows, with monitoring dashboards and governance controls your QA and IT teams can own independently.

Frequently asked questions

Will your agents work with our existing SAP / Oracle / Veeva setup, or do we need to change systems?

We build on top of your existing stack. The Wire stage is specifically about integrating agents into your live systems via APIs or RPA where APIs are not available. We have not yet encountered a pharma tech stack that required a core system replacement to make automation work.

How do you handle 21 CFR Part 11 and data integrity requirements for automated processes?

Every agent we deploy in a regulated workflow is designed with an audit trail by default — timestamped inputs, outputs, and decision logs. We align with your QA team during the Wire stage to ensure the automation layer meets your data integrity SOPs and does not introduce compliance gaps. For FDA-facing submissions, human review checkpoints are built into the workflow, not removed.

What does the Discovery audit actually produce?

A written report with three things: a map of your current workflow with manual touchpoints identified, a ranked list of the top 3 automation candidates with estimated time savings and implementation complexity for each, and a recommended pilot scope with a fixed-fee quote. You can take that report to any vendor — including competitors. Most clients proceed with us because the audit already shows we understand their operations.

Our IT team is stretched thin. How much internal bandwidth does this require from us?

Discovery requires roughly 4–6 hours of your team's time across interviews and workflow walkthroughs. The Pilot phase needs a designated internal contact (typically from QA or operations) for 2–3 hours per week. Full transformation requires more structured involvement, which we scope explicitly before the stage begins. We do not run open-ended engagements that quietly consume your team's calendar.

How long before we see actual ROI, not just a pilot report?

Most clients see measurable time savings within the 30-day Learn phase — typically 3–5 hours per week recovered per automated workflow. Full ROI payback on a Pilot engagement (₹4,50,000) usually occurs within 3–5 months based on the workflows we typically target in pharma. We will show you the math before you commit to the Pilot.
