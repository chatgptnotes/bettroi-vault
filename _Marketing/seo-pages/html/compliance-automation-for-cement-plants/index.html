Compliance Automation for Cement Plants Using AI Agents

Cement plants carry one of the heaviest compliance loads in Indian manufacturing. AI agents can automate most of it.

Bettroi builds and deploys AI agent systems that handle your CPCB/SPCB reporting, stack emission logs, consent-to-operate renewals, and internal audit trails — so your environment and safety teams stop doing data-entry and start doing actual work.

---

The Problem

A typical 2–5 MTPA cement plant generates dozens of compliance submissions every month across CPCB, SPCB, DGMS, and factory inspectorate channels — each with its own format, cadence, and penalty structure for non-compliance. Plant environment officers spend 60–70% of their time pulling data from SCADA, lab systems, and Excel sheets and reformatting it for government portals. One missed Continuous Ambient Air Quality Monitoring (CAAQM) upload or an incorrect stack emission figure can trigger a show-cause notice, and most plants have no automated alert when a deadline is 48 hours out.

---

What We Build for Cement Plants

  • Emission data aggregation agent — pulls SO₂, NOₓ, PM readings directly from SCADA/CEMS at configured intervals and structures them for CPCB/SPCB portal submission
  • Consent-to-operate renewal tracker — monitors expiry dates across all consents (air, water, hazardous waste), surfaces renewal tasks 90/60/30 days ahead, and drafts the application package
  • Monthly/quarterly compliance report bot — auto-generates EPR, environmental statement, and returns in the required government format using live plant data
  • DGMS and factory inspection readiness dashboard — consolidates safety registers, incident logs, and statutory records into a single audit-ready view
  • Non-conformance alert agent — compares live readings against permitted limits and fires alerts to the environment head and plant manager before a breach becomes a violation
  • Document version control system — maintains a timestamped, auditor-accessible archive of every submission, acknowledgement, and correspondence with regulatory bodies

How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We do not hand you a generic SaaS subscription and walk away. Every engagement runs through five stages:

1. Discovery (2 weeks) We audit your current compliance workflows — which teams touch which data, where submissions are built manually, where deadlines are tracked in someone's calendar. We identify the 3 highest-ROI automation candidates specific to your plant configuration and regulatory jurisdiction.

2. Learn (30 days) We deploy a pilot agent on one compliance workflow — typically emission reporting or consent tracking — and run it in parallel with your existing process. We measure baseline effort (person-hours per submission, error rate, missed deadlines) against the AI-assisted process.

3. Wire Winning agents are integrated into your actual systems: SCADA historians, lab LIMS, SAP/ERP for maintenance records, and government portal APIs where available. No more manual copy-paste between systems.

4. Automate Manual handoffs — environment officer → compliance head → plant manager sign-off → portal upload — are replaced with agent orchestration with human approval gates where required. Turnaround on a monthly SPCB return drops from 2 days to under 2 hours.

5. Scale The agent stack rolls out across all your units and plants, with a centralised compliance monitoring dashboard, role-based access, and governance controls that satisfy your internal audit team.

Frequently asked questions

Will your AI agents connect directly to our SCADA and CEMS systems, or do we have to export data manually?

We integrate directly with your SCADA historian and CEMS — OPC-UA, Modbus, and REST-based historian APIs are the most common paths we use in Indian industrial plants. In cases where a direct API connection is not available, we build a structured extraction layer so your team still does not touch the data manually. The exact integration approach is confirmed during the Discovery stage.

Which regulators and submission portals do your agents cover?

We currently cover CPCB and SPCB submission workflows (including the Parivesh portal), DGMS safety register requirements, and factory inspectorate documentation. We scope your specific state-level obligations during the Discovery audit, since SPCB requirements vary significantly between Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, and other major cement-belt states.

Our plant already uses SAP for some compliance tracking. Can you work with what we have instead of replacing it?

Yes. We wire our agents to your existing SAP modules rather than replacing them. SAP typically holds maintenance records, purchase data, and some statutory registers — our agents read from those tables and write outputs back where appropriate. We do not ask you to rip and replace; we add an orchestration layer on top.

How do you handle regulatory changes — for example, if CPCB revises its reporting format?

Regulatory format changes are covered under our retained AI operations subscription. When CPCB or an SPCB revises a portal format or reporting template, we update the relevant agent within 5 working days and push the change to your environment. If you are not on a retainer, we offer a fixed-fee update engagement per change event.

What does the 30-day pilot actually prove, and how do we know it is working?

During the pilot, we run the AI agent in parallel with your existing manual process on one workflow — typically your monthly SPCB returns or CEMS data submission. We track three metrics: person-hours spent per submission, number of data errors caught before submission, and on-time submission rate. At the end of 30 days you have a before-and-after comparison on all three, which becomes the business case for the full deployment.
