Document AI for Pharma Companies

Your documents are slowing your drug to market

A mid-size pharma company generates hundreds of controlled documents per product cycle — batch manufacturing records, regulatory dossiers, SOPs, pharmacovigilance reports, CMC sections, stability data summaries. Most of this is still assembled by hand: copy-paste from lab systems, manual QA review, version reconciliation across Word files. A single CDSCO or USFDA submission can take 6–10 weeks of document preparation work that could be compressed to days. Every week of delay in a submission or a batch release has a direct cost — in working capital, in regulatory timelines, and in competitive position.

---

The Problem

Pharma document workflows sit at the intersection of high compliance stakes and high manual effort — a combination that creates expensive backlogs. Regulatory affairs teams spend 40–60% of their time locating, formatting, and reconciling data rather than reviewing it. Batch manufacturing records (BMRs) are still transcribed by hand from SCADA or ERP outputs, introducing transcription errors that trigger deviations and delay batch release by days or weeks. And when an auditor or inspector asks for a specific document trail, assembling it takes days because records live across QMS, ERP, LIMS, and shared drives with no single retrieval layer.

  • BMR extraction and compilation agent — pulls structured data from your SCADA/ERP output and auto-populates batch manufacturing record templates, flagging out-of-spec values before human QA review

How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We do not drop a generic AI tool into your environment and call it a transformation. Every engagement follows five stages:

1. Discovery (2 weeks) We audit your current document workflows — BMR cycle, submission preparation, SOP control, PV case handling. We identify the 3 highest-ROI AI candidates based on volume, error rate, and downstream delay. You get a prioritised roadmap with effort and impact estimates before spending anything further.

2. Learn (30 days) We deploy a pilot agent on one workflow — typically the BMR extraction or dossier assembly use case — and run it in parallel with your existing process. We measure baseline cycle time, error rate, and reviewer hours versus the AI-assisted version. You see the delta in numbers, not slides.

3. Wire (weeks 5–8) Winning agents get integrated into your actual systems — SAP, Oracle, Veeva Vault, TrackWise, LIMS, or whatever your stack looks like. We build the connectors, data schemas, and access controls required for GxP-adjacent document workflows.

4. Automate (weeks 8–10) Manual handoffs between document creation, QA review, approval routing, and archival get replaced with orchestrated agent workflows. Humans stay in the loop at decision gates; everything else runs automatically.

5. Scale (weeks 10–12 and beyond) Roll out across product lines, manufacturing sites, or regulatory markets. We set up monitoring dashboards, drift detection, and audit logs so your QA team can demonstrate system control to any regulator.

Frequently asked questions

Is Document AI safe to use in a GxP-regulated pharma environment?

Yes, with the right architecture. AI agents in GxP workflows need to operate with full audit trails, human-in-the-loop review gates at decision points, and access controls that match your existing QMS permissions. We design every document AI system with these constraints as hard requirements, not afterthoughts. The agent never replaces a QA reviewer — it eliminates the manual assembly and retrieval work before and after that review.

Which systems can you integrate with — Veeva Vault, SAP, TrackWise?

Yes to all three, and also Oracle ERP, MasterControl, LIMS platforms like LabVantage or STARLIMS, and SCADA outputs. Integration complexity depends on whether your vendor exposes a proper API or whether we need to build a structured extraction layer. We scope this during Discovery so there are no surprises in the Wire stage.

How long before we see measurable results?

The Learn stage runs for 30 days on one workflow with baseline vs. AI measurement. Most clients see a 50–70% reduction in document assembly time on the pilot workflow within those 30 days. BMR compilation and dossier section drafting tend to show the largest gains because they involve the most repetitive data movement.

We have a CDSCO submission due in 3 months. Is that enough time?

A focused 6-week pilot (Discovery + Learn) can be completed with enough time to inform your submission process, but it will not fully automate it end-to-end in that window. What it will do is identify exactly where the time is being lost and give you a working agent on the highest-impact sub-task — for example, Module 3 CMC section drafting or stability summary generation. Full integration into your submission workflow happens in the Wire stage.

Do you work with generic pharma companies, or only large innovators?

Primarily generic and specialty pharma companies in the ₹100Cr–₹2,000Cr revenue range — these organisations have enough document volume to make automation ROI-positive but rarely have a dedicated AI team. API manufacturers and CROs operating under client QMS requirements are also a good fit. Large innovators with mature digital infrastructure are usually better served by their existing tech vendors.
