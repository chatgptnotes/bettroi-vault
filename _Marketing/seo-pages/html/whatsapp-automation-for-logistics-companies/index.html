How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We don't hand you a chatbot and disappear. Every engagement runs through five structured stages:

1. Discovery (Week 1–2) We audit your current WhatsApp workflows — shared inboxes, broadcast lists, manual forwarding chains. We map your TMS, ERP, or Google Sheets integrations. We identify the 3 highest-ROI automation candidates specific to your fleet size and customer mix.

2. Learn (Weeks 3–6) We deploy one agent — typically the shipment status bot — on a live corridor. We measure baseline response time, ops staff hours spent, and customer query repeat rate. At 30 days we have real numbers, not estimates.

3. Wire (Weeks 6–8) Winning agents get integrated into your core systems — TMS, FMS, or even a simple webhook into your existing tracking platform. WhatsApp Business API is configured with your number, templates approved by Meta, and conversation flows tested end-to-end.

4. Automate (Weeks 8–10) Manual handoffs — "someone update the customer", "remind the driver to upload POD" — are replaced by agent orchestration. Your ops team handles exceptions, not routine queries.

5. Scale (Weeks 10–12) Rollout across all lanes, depots, and customer accounts. Monitoring dashboards go live. Governance rules set (escalation thresholds, off-hours handling, fallback flows).

Frequently asked questions

We already use a WhatsApp Business account with a shared inbox tool. Why do we need AI agents on top of that?

Shared inbox tools (like Interakt, WATI, or AiSensy) give you visibility — they don't eliminate the manual work. Your team still reads every message and types or clicks a reply. AI agents actually respond, collect data, and trigger actions without a human in the loop. The shared inbox becomes your exception console, not your primary interface.

Will this work with our existing TMS / ERP, or do we need to switch systems?

We build integrations, not replacements. During Discovery we map your current stack — whether that's a commercial TMS like LogiNext or Locus, an in-house system, or even a Google Sheets-based tracker. Agents connect via API or webhook. If your system doesn't have an API, we can build a lightweight middleware layer. We've done this before.

How does WhatsApp Business API pricing work — is that extra?

Yes, WhatsApp Business API has its own conversation-based pricing charged by Meta directly — typically ₹0.25–₹0.58 per conversation depending on category (utility vs. marketing). We help you get onboarded, get templates approved, and estimate your monthly conversation volume so there are no surprises. Our fee covers build and integration; API usage is billed by Meta to your account.

Our drivers are not tech-savvy. Will they actually use this?

That's the right question. WhatsApp is already the tool your drivers use — that's the whole point. We design flows that require minimal input: a one-tap reply, a photo upload, or a location pin. No app downloads, no new logins. In our experience, driver adoption is higher for WhatsApp-native flows than for any dedicated driver app.

What happens when the bot can't handle a query?

Every agent we build has a defined escalation path. When a message hits a keyword threshold, a sentiment signal, or simply falls outside the bot's scope, it's flagged and routed to a human agent with the full conversation context already loaded. No customer gets a dead end. You define the escalation rules; we build them in from day one.
