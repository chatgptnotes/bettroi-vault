# AI Agents for Logistics Compliance Automation


## Compliance paperwork is killing your ops throughput

Logistics companies in India run on razor-thin margins and fat compliance stacks. Every shipment touches customs declarations, e-way bills, GST reconciliation, driver fitness certificates, vehicle permits, and freight invoices — most of it still handled by a coordinator copy-pasting across 4 portals. One missed document holds a truck at the border for 6 hours. One mis-filed e-way bill triggers a ₹10,000+ penalty. And your compliance team spends 60–70% of their week on data entry, not exception management.

## What we build for logistics compliance

- **E-way bill generation agent** — reads shipment data from your TMS/ERP and auto-generates, submits, and tracks e-way bills via NIC API, with expiry alerts built in
- **GST reconciliation bot** — matches GSTR-2B against purchase invoices daily, flags mismatches before the filing deadline, generates exception reports for your CA
- **Vehicle and driver compliance tracker** — monitors RC, fitness certificate, insurance, and driver licence expiry across your fleet; pushes renewal alerts 30/15/7 days out
- **Customs documentation checker** — validates Bill of Lading, packing list, and commercial invoice fields against shipper instructions before handoff to CHA
- **Audit trail dashboard** — every agent action logged with timestamp, user, source document, and output; ready for DGFT, GST, and internal audits
- **Compliance exception escalation workflow** — unresolved flags auto-escalate to ops manager via WhatsApp/email after 2 hours, with full context attached

## How we do it: the Be AI-First Framework

**Stage 1 — Discovery (2 weeks)**
We audit your current compliance workflows end to end: which documents, which portals, which teams, where the delays actually sit. We map the 3 highest-ROI automation candidates with baseline data — volume, error rate, time per task.

**Stage 2 — Learn (weeks 3–6)**
We deploy a pilot agent on one workflow — typically e-way bill generation or GST reconciliation — and run it in parallel with your existing process for 30 days. You see the numbers: processing time, error rate, coordinator hours saved.

**Stage 3 — Wire (weeks 7–8)**
Winning agents get integrated into your TMS, ERP (SAP/Tally/Zoho), and document management system. APIs, webhooks, or RPA bridges — whatever your stack needs.

**Stage 4 — Automate (weeks 9–10)**
Manual handoffs between compliance steps are replaced with agent orchestration. The coordinator stops filling forms and starts reviewing exceptions only.

**Stage 5 — Scale (weeks 11–12)**
Rollout across depots, lanes, or business units with monitoring dashboards and governance controls. New compliance rules (GST updates, CMVR amendments) get pushed as agent updates, not retraining cycles.

## Frequently asked questions

**Will the agents work with our existing TMS or ERP — we're on Tally and a custom freight system?**

Yes. Most logistics stacks we encounter are a mix of Tally, custom-built TMS, and government portals. Our Wire stage is specifically about integrating agents into whatever your current stack looks like — via APIs where available, RPA where not. We don't ask you to migrate or replace anything before going live.

**How do you handle GST rule changes or NIC portal updates without breaking the agents?**

This is exactly what the Retained AI Ops package covers. Regulatory changes — GST council updates, NIC API version changes, CMVR amendments — are pushed as agent updates, typically within 48–72 hours of the official notification. On the full transformation engagement, the first 3 months of updates are included.

**What happens when an agent makes an error on a compliance document?**

Every agent action produces a timestamped log with the source data, the output, and the confidence flag. Anything below threshold is held for human review — the agent doesn't auto-submit uncertain documents. In our pilot phase we run agents in shadow mode first, so you see the error rate before any live submission happens.

**We have 12 depots across 4 states. Can this scale across all of them?**

That's the Scale stage. After the pilot proves the model works on one workflow or one depot, we roll out with depot-specific configurations — each location can have its own state GST rules, permit types, and escalation contacts — while the central dashboard gives your compliance head a single view across all sites.

**How long before we see actual time savings on the ground?**

Typically within the first 2 weeks of the Learn stage, coordinators report a noticeable drop in manual entry time on the piloted workflow. By the end of the 6-week pilot, we produce a before/after report with exact hours saved per week and document error rates. Most clients see 50–65% reduction in time spent on the automated workflow.
