# HMS Modernization for Logistics Companies

## Your HMS was built for a different decade

Logistics operations run 24/7 across depots, drivers, and dispatch desks — but most Health Management Systems in the sector still run on spreadsheets, WhatsApp chains, and whoever picks up the phone first. Driver medical compliance gets missed. Fleet health data sits in silos. Billing aging reports take three people two days to produce. When your HMS can't keep pace with your fleet, every manual handoff is a delay — or a liability.

## What we build for logistics companies

- **Driver health compliance agent** — flags overdue medicals, licence renewals, and fitness certificates automatically, 72 hours before expiry
- **Fleet health dashboard bot** — pulls service records, breakdown history, and tyre/oil cycle data into a single daily digest sent to the ops manager
- **Bill aging automation module** — generates vendor and client bill aging reports on a fixed schedule, replacing the manual Excel pull (built and live on Adamrit HMS)
- **Dispatch documentation bot** — auto-populates route manifests, driver allocation sheets, and trip closure documents from a single source of truth
- **Regulatory audit pack generator** — compiles compliance data (transport permits, pollution certificates, insurance) into audit-ready reports, reducing prep time from days to minutes
- **Incident and claims triage agent** — captures accident/breakdown reports via WhatsApp or web form, routes to legal/insurance team with pre-filled claim fields

## How we do it: the Be AI-First Framework

**Stage 1 — Discovery (2 weeks)**
We audit your current HMS, dispatch, and compliance workflows end-to-end. You get a written report naming the 3 highest-ROI AI automation candidates specific to your operation — not a generic deck.

**Stage 2 — Learn (weeks 3–6)**
We run one AI agent on your live workflow and measure it against your baseline for 30 days. Dispatch documentation bot or bill aging agent — whichever hurts most right now. You see the delta in numbers before committing further.

**Stage 3 — Wire (weeks 7–9)**
Winning agents are integrated into your existing HMS, CRM, or ERP via API or direct DB connection. No rip-and-replace. Your team keeps the tools they know; the agents do the grunt work underneath.

**Stage 4 — Automate (weeks 9–11)**
Manual handoffs — the "send to ops manager on WhatsApp", the "remind HR about the medical", the "pull the aging report Friday morning" — get replaced with orchestrated agent workflows with audit trails.

**Stage 5 — Scale (week 12 onwards)**
We roll out across depots or verticals with a monitoring dashboard and governance layer so your ops head can see what every agent is doing and override when needed.

## Frequently asked questions

**We already have an HMS. Does modernization mean replacing it?**

No. We wire AI agents into your existing HMS via API or direct database integration. Your team keeps the same interface. The agents handle the data collection, report generation, and compliance flagging underneath — no rip-and-replace required.

**How long before we see any measurable output?**

The Discovery audit delivers a written ROI report in 2 weeks. If you move to the Pilot stage, you have a live AI agent running on your workflow within 6 weeks, with baseline vs. AI performance numbers at the end of the 30-day run.

**Our driver compliance and fleet data are in three different systems. Can agents still work?**

Yes — this is the normal state we walk into. The Wire stage specifically maps and connects your disparate data sources. We've done this across HMS, CRM, and ERP environments. Fragmented source data is a scoping factor, not a blocker.

**What happens if an agent makes a wrong call — flags the wrong driver or misses a renewal?**

Every agent we build has a human-in-the-loop override layer and a full audit trail. The agent flags and routes; a human confirms before any consequential action is taken. The monitoring dashboard built in Stage 5 lets your ops head see every agent decision in real time.

**Is this relevant for a mid-size fleet of 80-150 trucks, or only for large enterprises?**

Mid-size fleets are actually the best fit. You have enough operational complexity to generate real ROI from automation, but you're not carrying the legacy IT bureaucracy that slows enterprise deployments. Most of our pilots deliver payback within the first 90 days for operations this size.
