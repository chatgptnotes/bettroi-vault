# AI Workflow Automation for Manufacturing Plants

## Your plant runs on manual handoffs. That is the bottleneck.

Shift supervisors reconcile production orders against inventory in spreadsheets. Procurement teams chase suppliers on WhatsApp. MRP outputs sit in SAP until someone reads them on Monday morning. By the time the right person sees the right number, the line has already slowed down or a rush purchase has already been raised.

Bettroi builds AI agents that sit inside your existing systems — SAP, SCADA, your ERP — and close these loops automatically, without replacing your team or your processes overnight.

---

## The Problem

Manual workflow handoffs between planning, procurement, and shop-floor are the single biggest source of unplanned downtime and inventory waste in mid-size manufacturing plants. Production schedules generated by MRP are stale within hours because no agent is watching real-time stock movements and open orders simultaneously. Buyers act on yesterday's data; supervisors raise verbal escalations that never get logged; root-cause analysis after a line stoppage takes three days of email archaeology.

---

## What We Build

- **Master Production Schedule (MPS) agent** — runs MPS, MRP, and daily scheduling as one integrated loop every night and on event trigger, pulling live data from SAP `ProductionOrder` and `StockLevel` via OData or scheduled CSV export
- **JIT procurement agent** — monitors reorder points, auto-drafts purchase requisitions, and flags supplier lead-time violations before a stockout hits the line
- **Shop-floor handoff bot** — converts shift-end verbal summaries into structured logs, routes exceptions to the right supervisor, and closes the loop in your ERP
- **Bottleneck detection agent** — applies Theory of Constraints drum-buffer-rope logic to flag the current constraint shift-by-shift, not week-by-week
- **Batch record and compliance doc bot** — for process/pharma/food plants running 21-CFR-11 or similar, auto-populates batch records from SCADA events and flags CIP/SIP deviations
- **Planning correction loop** — captures every manual override a planner makes, learns the reasoning pattern over 30 days, and reduces the override rate by incorporating those rules into the next scheduling cycle

## How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We do not sell a product. We run a structured engagement that ends with agents running in production.

**Stage 1 — Discovery (2 weeks)**
We audit your current workflows end-to-end: planning cycle, procurement triggers, shop-floor handoffs, ERP data quality. We identify the 3 workflows where an AI agent will return measurable ROI fastest — typically MPS automation, procurement alerting, or shift-log digitisation.

**Stage 2 — Learn (weeks 3–6)**
We deploy a pilot agent on one workflow and run it in parallel with your existing process for 30 days. We measure baseline (time spent, error rate, exception volume) vs. agent output. No go-live risk. If the numbers do not move, we tell you.

**Stage 3 — Wire (weeks 7–9)**
Winning agents get integrated into your core systems. SAP integration starts with scheduled OData exports or CSV — we do not ask your IT team to open API access on day one. SCADA hooks follow where available.

**Stage 4 — Automate (weeks 10–11)**
Manual handoffs are replaced by agent orchestration. Procurement requisitions, shift reports, schedule updates — agents handle the routine; your team handles exceptions only.

**Stage 5 — Scale (week 12+)**
Rollout to additional lines, shifts, or plants. Monitoring dashboards go live. Governance rules are set so plant managers can audit every agent decision.

## Frequently asked questions

**We already have SAP with MRP running. Why do we need an AI agent on top?**

SAP MRP is a batch process — it runs on a schedule and produces a plan based on the data at that moment. It does not watch for real-time deviations, supplier delays, or shop-floor exceptions and re-plan automatically. An AI agent runs continuously, detects when actual conditions diverge from the plan, and either corrects the schedule or escalates to the right person before the line is affected. The agent does not replace SAP — it makes SAP's outputs actionable in near real-time.

**How do you integrate with our systems without disrupting ongoing production?**

We start with read-only scheduled data exports — SAP OData queries or CSV drops to a shared folder. No API access required on day one, no IT change-control risk. The pilot agent runs in parallel with your existing process for 30 days. Integration deepens only after the pilot proves value and your IT team is comfortable with the approach.

**How long before we see actual results, not just a report?**

The Discovery audit (2 weeks) gives you a prioritised workflow map and a concrete ROI estimate per candidate — that is a usable output on its own. The Pilot stage (weeks 3–6) puts a live agent on one workflow and measures it against your baseline. Most clients see quantifiable output — time saved, exception rate reduced, or purchase requisition lead time cut — within the first 30 days of the pilot.

**Our plant has pharma / food compliance requirements. Can your agents handle that?**

Yes. Our agent architecture includes pre-loaded skills for 21-CFR-11 batch record compliance, CIP/SIP validation rules between batches, and grade-transition management. The batch record bot auto-populates records from SCADA events and flags deviations before a batch is closed. We have built this specifically for process manufacturing plants in pharma, food, and specialty chemicals.

**What does the engagement look like for a plant with multiple lines or multiple sites?**

The standard 12-week full transformation covers one plant or one primary production line cluster. Multi-site rollout happens in Stage 5 (Scale), where winning agents are replicated with site-specific configuration — different BOMs, supplier lists, shift structures. Governance dashboards let plant managers at each site audit agent decisions independently. Pricing for multi-site scale is scoped after Stage 2 data is in hand.
