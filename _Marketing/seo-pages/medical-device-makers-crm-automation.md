# CRM Automation for Medical Device Makers

## Your sales team is doing data entry. Your CRM is not closing deals — it is collecting dust.

Medical device sales cycles are long, multi-stakeholder, and document-heavy. A rep manages hospital procurement officers, biomedical engineers, surgeons, and compliance teams — often across 10-15 accounts simultaneously. Most of that context lives in WhatsApp threads and Excel sheets, not in the CRM. When a rep leaves, the account history walks out with them.

---

## The Problem

Medical device field reps spend 30–40% of their week on CRM update tasks — logging calls, attaching quotations, updating deal stages — instead of being in front of buyers. Sterilization and implant categories (pacemakers, stents, surgical instruments) carry regulatory documentation requirements that rarely get tracked in CRM consistently. And because procurement decisions at hospitals involve 4–6 approvers, follow-up sequences fall apart the moment one handoff is missed.

---

## What We Build

- **Lead intake agent** — captures inbound enquiries from email, web forms, and WhatsApp, creates CRM records with correct product category tags automatically
- **Account activity logger** — listens to call transcripts and meeting notes, writes structured CRM updates without the rep typing a single field
- **Multi-stakeholder follow-up sequencer** — tracks each approver (procurement, biomedical, clinical) separately and triggers the right follow-up message at the right stage
- **Quotation and compliance document tracker** — links sent quotations, CE/MDR certificates, and CDSCO documentation to the correct deal record and flags expiry
- **Deal stall detector** — flags deals with no activity in 14+ days and surfaces recommended next actions to the regional manager
- **Handover pack generator** — when a rep is reassigned, auto-generates a structured account brief from all CRM history, call logs, and document trails

## Frequently asked questions

**We already have Zoho CRM. Do we need to switch tools?**

No. We build agents that work on top of your existing CRM — Zoho, Salesforce, HubSpot, or custom. We use APIs and webhooks to wire agents in without migrating your data or retraining your team on a new interface.

**Our field reps are not technical. Will they actually use this?**

The agent does the work, not the rep. The activity logger pulls from call transcripts and WhatsApp — the rep does not fill in any form. The follow-up sequencer runs in the background. We specifically design for zero-friction adoption because a tool that reps avoid is useless.

**How do you handle CDSCO and MDR compliance documentation in the CRM?**

The quotation and compliance document tracker agent links regulatory certificates to the correct deal record and flags documents approaching expiry. During Discovery, we map your specific compliance document set and build the tracking logic around it.

**What happens to our CRM data during the pilot?**

We work with a scoped dataset — typically one region or one product line — during the 30-day Learn stage. Your full CRM data is not touched until Stage 3 (Wire), and we sign an NDA and data processing agreement before any access is granted.

**How long before we see measurable results?**

The Learn stage is specifically designed to produce a measurable before/after comparison within 30 days. Typical early metrics we track: CRM update time per rep per week, follow-up compliance rate, and average days-to-next-action on stalled deals.
