# Predictive Analytics for Medical Device Makers

## Device failures don't announce themselves. Your data already knows.

Bettroi builds predictive analytics systems for medical device manufacturers — so your quality, supply chain, and service teams catch problems before regulators, hospitals, or patients do.

---

## The Problem

Medical device manufacturers sit on dense sensor, batch, and field-service data but almost none of it is wired into a live decision loop — it gets reviewed after a complaint or a recall trigger. Demand forecasting for implants and consumables (pacemakers, stents, sterilisation equipment) is still largely spreadsheet-driven, which means either overstock tying up capital or stockouts that delay surgeries. And with CDSCO, MDR, and ISO 13485 audits growing stricter, traceability gaps discovered late cost ten times more to fix than if they had been flagged at the process step.

---

## What We Build for You

- **Batch quality prediction agent** — scores each production batch in real time using sensor and process parameters; flags batches at risk of failing QC before they reach final inspection
- **Field-failure early-warning model** — ingests post-market surveillance data, service tickets, and hospital feedback to predict device failure clusters 30–60 days out
- **Demand forecasting pipeline** — SKU-level predictions for implants and consumables, updated weekly, integrated into your ERP or inventory system
- **Supplier risk scorecard agent** — monitors supplier lead times, reject rates, and sterilisation compliance (relevant especially for contract sterilisation partners) to surface sourcing risk before it hits your line
- **Regulatory traceability bot** — auto-generates batch genealogy reports and deviation summaries formatted for CDSCO / MDR submissions, pulling from your MES or vault
- **Executive analytics dashboard** — a single-pane view of predicted yield, field risk, and inventory health; updated daily, no manual assembly required

## How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We do not hand you a generic ML platform and walk away. Every engagement follows five stages:

**1. Discovery (Week 1–2)**
Two-week audit of your production workflows, quality data, and field-service records. We identify the three highest-ROI prediction targets — typically batch quality, demand accuracy, and field-failure rate. Fixed fee, standalone deliverable.

**2. Learn (Weeks 3–8)**
We deploy a pilot agent on one workflow and run it in parallel with your current process for 30 days. You see baseline vs. AI performance in hard numbers — reject rate delta, forecast MAPE, or service-call reduction — before committing to full integration.

**3. Wire (Weeks 9–14)**
Winning agents are integrated into your core systems — MES, ERP, QMS, or SCADA. Data flows automatically; no one has to export a CSV.

**4. Automate (Weeks 15–18)**
Manual handoffs — QC sign-offs, reorder triggers, deviation report drafts — are replaced by agent-orchestrated workflows. Your team reviews exceptions, not routine cases.

**5. Scale (Weeks 19–24)**
Rollout across product lines, manufacturing sites, or geographies, with monitoring dashboards and governance controls that satisfy ISO 13485 audit requirements.

## Frequently asked questions

**We already have a BI tool (Power BI / Tableau). Why do we need Bettroi?**

BI tools show you what happened. Predictive analytics agents tell you what is about to happen — a batch likely to fail QC, a device cluster approaching end-of-life in the field, a supplier about to miss lead time. The two are complementary; we typically integrate our agents' outputs into your existing BI layer so your team sees predictions alongside historical data in one place.

**Our data is messy and spread across an old MES, Excel files, and a service CRM. Can you still build this?**

Yes. Messy, fragmented data is the norm in medical device manufacturing in India, not the exception. The Discovery audit specifically maps your data sources, identifies what is usable, and flags the gaps. We have worked with Obsidian vaults, legacy MES exports, and ad-hoc spreadsheets as source inputs before productionising pipelines.

**How do you handle ISO 13485 and CDSCO traceability requirements?**

Our regulatory traceability bot is built to generate batch genealogy reports and deviation summaries in formats aligned with ISO 13485 clause 8.3 and CDSCO technical file requirements. The agents are grounded on your own SOPs (not generic templates), and every agent action is logged with a timestamp and source reference for audit purposes.

**What does the 30-day pilot actually measure?**

We agree on a primary metric before the pilot starts — typically one of: QC first-pass yield improvement, forecast MAPE reduction, or field-service call prediction accuracy. At day 30 you get a one-page results report showing baseline vs. AI on that metric. If the improvement does not justify proceeding, we say so — no pressure to continue.

**Do you work with contract manufacturers or only OEMs?**

Both. For OEMs the priority is usually batch quality prediction and demand forecasting. For contract manufacturers the most valuable use case is typically supplier risk monitoring and sterilisation compliance tracking — areas we have direct exposure to through our work with sterilisation-services companies in the medical device supply chain.
