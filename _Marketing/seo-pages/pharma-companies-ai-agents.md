## The Problem

Mid-to-large pharma companies run on document-heavy, approval-gated workflows that were designed for human routing — not speed. Your regulatory affairs team is copy-pasting data between CTMS, eDMS, and submission portals. Your pharmacovigilance desk is manually triaging adverse event reports that arrive in three formats from five channels. And every batch-release cycle involves someone chasing sign-offs over WhatsApp because the system doesn't talk to the people. These are not process problems. They are automation gaps — and they compound every quarter.

## What We Build

- **Regulatory submission agent** — monitors dossier status across CDSCO, EU-CTR, and internal eDMS; flags gaps and drafts cover letters from templates
- **Adverse event (AE) intake and triage bot** — parses incoming AE reports from email, web forms, and CRM exports; classifies by seriousness and routes to the correct PV officer
- **Batch-release documentation bot** — pulls batch manufacturing records, QC sign-off data, and deviation logs; compiles release dossier draft for QA review
- **Medical representative (MR) CRM automation agent** — logs field visit summaries, updates doctor contact records, and triggers follow-up sequences without MR manual entry
- **Supply chain exception agent** — watches inventory and demand signals across warehouses; alerts procurement and auto-drafts POs when reorder thresholds are hit
- **SOD and SOP compliance monitoring agent** — cross-checks batch and process logs against active SOPs; surfaces deviations before the audit window closes

## Frequently asked questions

**We already use an ERP and a CRM. Will your agents work on top of those or replace them?**

On top of them. We integrate agents into your existing stack — SAP, Oracle, Salesforce, Zoho, whatever you run. The agent reads from and writes to your systems via API or RPA where APIs are not available. We do not ask you to migrate anything.

**How do you handle 21 CFR Part 11 or CDSCO audit-trail requirements for AI-generated documents?**

Every agent action is logged with a timestamp, the input data it acted on, and the output it produced. For regulated document workflows, we build a human-in-the-loop review step so the final submission or release document always carries a human sign-off. The agent does the drafting; your qualified person approves. That is the same structure regulators already accept for template-assisted authoring.

**What is the minimum company size this makes sense for?**

If you have a dedicated regulatory affairs or PV team of 5 or more people, or a sales force of 30 or more MRs, there is almost certainly a workflow that justifies a pilot. Below that, we will tell you in the Discovery audit if the ROI is not there — we would rather lose a small deal than sell you something that does not pay back.

**How long before we see actual time savings?**

In the Learn stage, which runs 30 days, you get a direct before-and-after comparison on the pilot workflow. Most clients see 40–70% reduction in cycle time on the specific task the agent handles. Full-org impact takes longer — typically 3 to 6 months post-Wire stage — because change management inside pharma companies is not fast, and we do not pretend otherwise.

**Do you sign NDAs and are you open to working under our data security policies?**

Yes on both. We sign mutual NDAs before the Discovery audit begins. We work within your data security policies and can deploy agents inside your own cloud environment (AWS, Azure, GCP) if you do not want data leaving your perimeter. For on-premise requirements, we assess feasibility during Discovery.
