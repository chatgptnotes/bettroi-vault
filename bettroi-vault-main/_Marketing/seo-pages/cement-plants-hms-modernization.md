# HMS Modernization for Cement Plants

## Most cement plant HMS setups are a liability, not a system

Your occupational health centre runs on paper registers, Excel exports, and a standalone software that nobody has touched since the last statutory audit. The plant medical officer is manually compiling Form 7, Form 14, and periodic medical examination (PME) records every quarter — hours of work that produce data nobody acts on. When a dust-exposure incident happens, it takes two days to pull a worker's full health history.

That is not an HMS. That is a documentation backlog with a login screen.

---

## What we build for cement plant HMS modernization

- **PME scheduling agent** — auto-generates periodic medical examination schedules per worker category (kiln, packing, quarry), sends reminders, and flags overdue examinations to the safety officer
- **Occupational health record digitizer** — ingests legacy paper forms and existing Excel data into a structured worker health profile; links exposure history to medical findings
- **Statutory compliance dashboard** — live tracking of Factory Act and Mines Act reporting obligations (Form 7, Form 14, Form 21), with auto-drafted submissions ready for MO sign-off
- **Dust and noise exposure correlation module** — maps SCADA-sourced exposure readings against worker health records to surface early-warning patterns before they become compensation claims
- **Incident-to-record bot** — when a first-aid or OHC visit is logged, automatically updates the worker's health record, notifies the safety team, and initiates the statutory reporting workflow if thresholds are crossed
- **MIS reporting agent** — weekly and monthly occupational health MIS to plant head and corporate EHS, zero manual compilation

**Our plant already has an HMS software. Do we need to replace it?**

No. We build agents that sit on top of your existing HMS and fill the gaps — scheduling, reporting, SCADA correlation — without touching your core software license or data structure. Rip-and-replace is slower and more expensive; we avoid it.

**How do you handle Factory Act and Mines Act compliance requirements specific to cement manufacturing?**

The Discovery audit explicitly maps your statutory obligations — Form 7, Form 14, Form 21, PME frequency by worker category — against your current documentation gaps. The compliance dashboard we build is configured to your specific regulatory calendar, not a generic template.

**We have workers across 2-3 plant locations. Can this scale across sites?**

Yes, multi-site rollout is Stage 5 of our framework. The pilot always runs on one location first so you have proof of ROI before we replicate. Corporate EHS dashboards with cross-site visibility are a standard deliverable in the full transformation engagement.

**What does the 30-day pilot actually measure? How do we know if it's working?**

Before the pilot starts, we establish a baseline: time taken to compile a monthly PME status report, number of overdue examinations, time to retrieve a worker's health history on request. After 30 days on the AI agent, we measure the same numbers. You get a one-page before/after comparison — not a demo, actual operational data from your plant.

**Our plant IT team is conservative about third-party integrations. How do you handle that?**

We scope IT constraints explicitly during Discovery. Most cement plant deployments we plan for are air-gap-friendly or work within the plant's existing VPN and firewall setup. We can operate with read-only SCADA access and do not require write access to production systems unless the client specifically wants it.
