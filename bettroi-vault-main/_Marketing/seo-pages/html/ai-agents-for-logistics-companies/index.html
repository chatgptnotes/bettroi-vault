The Problem

Logistics operations generate hundreds of micro-decisions per day — vehicle allocation, route deviation alerts, invoice matching, customer status updates — and most companies are handling them through a combination of phone calls, group chats, and manual dashboards. When shipment volume scales, this breaks: coordinators get overwhelmed, SLA breaches go undetected until the customer calls, and invoicing backlogs pile up into week-long reconciliation cycles. The data exists in your systems — the problem is no one has wired it into agents that act on it automatically.

How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We don't do open-ended AI consulting. Every engagement follows five stages with defined exits at each one.

1. Discovery (2 weeks) We audit your current dispatch, tracking, invoicing, and customer communication workflows. You get a ranked list of the 3 highest-ROI AI candidates — specific to your shipment volumes, tech stack, and team structure. Fixed fee, no commitment beyond this stage.

2. Learn (30 days) We deploy a pilot agent on one workflow — typically ETA monitoring or invoice reconciliation — and run it in parallel with your existing process. At the end of 30 days, you have hard numbers: baseline manual effort vs. agent-handled volume, error rate, and time saved.

3. Wire The winning agents get integrated into your actual systems — TMS, ERP, WhatsApp Business API, GPS platform, or custom fleet management software. No standalone tools sitting outside your stack.

4. Automate Manual handoffs get replaced with agent orchestration. Coordinator checks-in become exception-only. Reporting that took 2 hours gets generated in 2 minutes.

5. Scale We roll out across depots, lanes, or business units with monitoring dashboards and governance protocols so your ops head has visibility into what every agent is doing and why.

Frequently asked questions

Our TMS is custom-built and not a standard product. Can your agents still integrate with it?

Yes. We've integrated with custom-built systems before — as long as your TMS exposes an API, a database we can query, or even a webhook, we can wire agents into it. During the Discovery audit we map your actual integration surface and flag anything that needs custom connector work before we quote the pilot.

We already have a GPS tracking platform. What does an AI agent add on top of that?

GPS platforms give you visibility — they show you where vehicles are. An AI agent acts on that data: it compares live position against planned schedule, calculates breach probability for each active shipment, sends proactive customer notifications, and escalates to your ops manager before the SLA is missed. The platform gives you data; the agent converts data into action without a human in the loop.

How long before we see ROI from a pilot?

The Learn stage is 30 days by design — long enough to get a statistically meaningful read on agent-handled volume versus manual effort. Most logistics pilots show measurable time savings by week 2. Whether that translates to a full transformation investment depends on your volumes and coordinator costs, which we calculate for you explicitly at the end of the pilot.

Do we need to hire AI or ML talent internally to work with your agents?

No. Bettroi builds, deploys, and maintains the agents. Your ops team interacts with the outputs — alerts, reports, escalations — not the underlying models. If you want to build internal capability over time, we can structure the engagement to include knowledge transfer, but it's not a prerequisite.

What happens after the 12-week full transformation? Are we on our own?

You can take over operations yourself — we document everything and hand over fully. Most clients opt for our retained AI operations package (from ₹60,000/month) so we continue monitoring agent performance, handling model drift, and expanding automation as new workflows come up. Either way, you're not locked in after the transformation engagement ends.
