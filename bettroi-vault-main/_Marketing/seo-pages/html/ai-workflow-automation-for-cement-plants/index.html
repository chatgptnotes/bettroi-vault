The Problem

Cement plants run on shift handover notes, WhatsApp forwards, and spreadsheets that no one fully trusts. Quality lab results sit in one system, kiln parameters in another, and dispatch commitments in a third — and your plant manager is the human middleware reconciling all three at 7 AM every day. When something goes wrong — a clinker quality deviation, a crusher breakdown, a truck queue pileup at the weigh bridge — the delay is not the event itself, it is the 40 minutes of calls and cross-checks before anyone acts. That coordination cost runs silently across every shift, every day, in every plant.

---

What We Build

  • Shift handover agent — pulls data from SCADA, DCS, and lab LIMS, auto-generates a structured shift report, and flags open action items before the outgoing crew signs off
  • Kiln deviation alert workflow — monitors thermal profile and free-lime readings, triggers a structured escalation message to the CCR operator and plant head when thresholds are breached, with suggested corrective actions logged
  • Dispatch planning bot — cross-references clinker inventory, grinding capacity, and pending customer orders to generate a daily dispatch plan, reducing manual back-and-forth between plant and marketing teams
  • Preventive maintenance ticket agent — reads running-hour counters and vibration sensor feeds, raises SAP PM tickets automatically when thresholds are hit, and tracks closure status
  • P&ID and SOP extraction tool — parses your existing P&ID drawings and SOPs into a searchable knowledge base that operators and maintenance teams can query in plain language (built on technology we have already developed for industrial plants)
  • Daily MIS automation — compiles production, power consumption, and quality KPIs from multiple sources into a formatted report delivered to leadership by 8 AM, without a single person manually pulling data

How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We do not hand you a platform license and disappear. Every engagement runs through five stages:

1. Discovery (2 weeks) We audit your current workflows across CCR, quality lab, logistics, and maintenance. We identify the 3 workflows where AI automation delivers the fastest, most measurable ROI — and we tell you which ones are not worth automating yet.

2. Learn (30 days) We run a live pilot on one workflow — say, shift handover automation. We measure baseline time, error rate, and escalation lag against the AI-assisted version. You see real numbers before committing further.

3. Wire The winning agent gets integrated into your actual systems — SAP, your SCADA historian, your LIMS, your weighbridge software. No parallel manual process running alongside.

4. Automate Manual handoffs are replaced with agent orchestration. Approvals, alerts, escalations, and reports flow without human middleware.

5. Scale We roll out across multiple production lines or plant locations, with monitoring dashboards and governance controls so your IT and ops teams stay in full control.

Frequently asked questions

Our plant already runs SAP and a SCADA historian. Do your agents work with existing systems or do we need new software?

We integrate with what you have. Our agents connect to SAP PM and MM modules, standard SCADA historians (OSIsoft PI, Ignition, Wonderware), and most LIMS platforms via API or database connectors. We do not ask you to replace any core system during a pilot — the agent sits on top and reads from your existing data sources.

How long before we see actual results, not just a prototype?

In the Pilot engagement, you have a live agent running on a real workflow within 6 weeks. We measure against your own baseline — time saved per shift, reduction in escalation lag, or report generation time — and give you a written ROI summary at the end of 30 days of live operation.

Our shift data and quality records are messy and inconsistent. Is our data good enough for AI automation?

Messy data is the norm in cement plants, not the exception. During Discovery, we assess data quality as part of the audit. In most cases, the automation itself imposes structure on data collection going forward — the shift handover agent, for instance, prompts operators to fill in structured fields rather than free-text notes. We are explicit about what data gaps will limit results before you commit to a pilot.

Who maintains the agents after go-live? We do not have an in-house AI team.

Most of our clients do not have in-house AI teams — that is precisely why they work with us. Our Retained AI Ops subscription covers monitoring, retraining when process parameters change, bug fixes, and adding new workflows. You get a single point of contact, not a support ticket queue.

What is the minimum plant size or production volume where this makes financial sense?

As a rough guide, if your plant produces upwards of 1,000 TPD and you have more than two shifts with separate supervisory teams, the coordination overhead alone justifies a Discovery audit. Smaller plants with complex logistics or multi-product lines can also benefit. The Discovery audit itself will tell you whether the ROI clears the bar — if it does not, we will say so.
