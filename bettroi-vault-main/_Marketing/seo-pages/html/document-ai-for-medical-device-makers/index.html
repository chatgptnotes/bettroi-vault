Your documentation burden is a production risk

Medical device manufacturers run on paper — or worse, on PDFs scattered across shared drives. A single 510(k) submission pulls from Design History Files, risk management reports, biocompatibility data, and supplier declarations that live in five different systems, maintained by three different teams. One missing revision, one mislabelled document version, and your submission stalls or your audit fails. What we build for medical device makers

  • DHF assembly agent — automatically pulls design inputs, outputs, verification records, and validation reports into a structured Design History File, version-controlled and audit-ready
  • 510(k) / CE Technical File drafting bot — ingests your internal test data and risk files, drafts submission-ready sections with correct regulatory cross-references
  • Supplier document tracker — monitors incoming certificates of conformance, material declarations, and ISO certs; flags expiry and missing documents before they block production
  • Change control documentation agent — converts engineering change requests into formatted ECO/ECN packets with traceability links to affected DHF sections
  • Complaint and CAPA summariser — reads incoming complaint records, extracts structured fields, and pre-populates CAPA templates for QA review
  • SOP version-control assistant — detects SOP conflicts, flags outdated references, and routes approval workflows with a full audit trail

How we do it: the Be AI-First Framework

Stage 1 — Discovery (2 weeks) We audit your current document workflows end-to-end: where files live, who touches them, how long each step takes, and where submissions get delayed. We identify the 3 highest-ROI document AI candidates specific to your product lines and regulatory markets (US FDA, EU MDR, CDSCO).

Stage 2 — Learn (weeks 3–6) We deploy a pilot agent on one workflow — typically DHF assembly or supplier document tracking — and run it in parallel with your existing process for 30 days. We measure baseline hours vs. AI-assisted hours and error rates. You see the delta before committing further.

Stage 3 — Wire (weeks 7–9) Winning agents get integrated into your actual systems — your QMS (MasterControl, Veeva, or a custom setup), ERP, or document management platform. No rip-and-replace; we wire into what you already run.

Stage 4 — Automate (weeks 10–11) Manual handoffs between QA, regulatory affairs, and engineering get replaced with agent orchestration. A change request raised in engineering automatically triggers the right document update, review routing, and DHF cross-reference — without a human coordinator in the middle.

Stage 5 — Scale Roll out across product lines, manufacturing sites, and regulatory markets. We add monitoring dashboards, SLA alerts, and governance controls so your quality team stays in command.

Frequently asked questions

We already have a QMS like MasterControl or Veeva. Does your Document AI replace it?

No. We wire our agents into your existing QMS — they read from it, write to it, and trigger workflows inside it. We are not a QMS vendor. Your validated system stays in place; we remove the manual work that sits around it.

How does this handle 21 CFR Part 11 or EU MDR audit trail requirements?

Every agent action is logged with a timestamp, user context, and document version reference. We design the audit trail into the architecture from day one, not as an afterthought. We will review your specific regulatory requirements during the Discovery audit and confirm the approach before the Pilot begins.

Our product documentation has a lot of proprietary IP. How is data handled?

We can deploy the entire stack inside your own cloud environment (AWS, Azure, or GCP tenancy) or on-premise if required. No proprietary data leaves your infrastructure. Data handling and confidentiality terms are documented in the engagement agreement before kickoff.

What document formats and sources can you ingest?

PDFs, Word documents, Excel-based test logs, CAD-linked metadata exports, and structured QMS records. If it has text in it, we can build an ingestion pipeline for it. Scanned paper documents with poor OCR quality are the one case where we will tell you upfront what the accuracy ceiling is.

How long before we see actual time savings?

The Learn stage runs the pilot agent for 30 days in parallel with your existing process. By the end of week six, you will have a measured hours-saved figure for that specific workflow. Most device maker pilots show 40–60% time reduction on the target document process. Full transformation savings compound across Stage 4 as manual handoffs are eliminated.
