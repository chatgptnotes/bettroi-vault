Frequently asked questions

Our ERP data is messy and our LIMS exports are inconsistent. Can you still build predictive models on it?

Yes — messy source data is the norm, not the exception. During Discovery we assess data completeness, gap patterns, and whether gaps are systematic or random. Most pharma ERPs have enough historical signal even with missing fields. We build data cleaning pipelines as part of the Wire stage. If the data is genuinely too sparse to model reliably, we tell you that in week two rather than six months in.

How is this different from the analytics module already in our SAP or Oracle system?

Built-in ERP analytics are backward-looking reporting tools — they tell you what happened. We build forward-looking models that are trained on your specific SKU mix, your specific supplier behaviour, and your own deviation history. An SAP demand report averages the past. A trained forecasting agent learns the patterns in your data and updates its predictions as new data arrives.

Do we need a data science team in-house to run this after you're done?

No. We build for the planning manager and the QA head, not for data scientists. Outputs are dashboards, alerts, and exception queues in tools your team already uses — email, WhatsApp, or a web dashboard. Model retraining and drift monitoring are covered under the retained operations subscription. You can also hand off to an internal team if you develop one later — we document everything.

What does 'batch failure prediction' actually mean in practice? We can't stop a batch mid-process.

You can intervene earlier than you think. Batch failure prediction flags parameter drift during in-process stages — granulation moisture, blending time deviations, temperature excursions — before the final QC check. In practice this means QA gets an alert at the 60% mark of a batch run rather than at the end-of-batch review. That window is enough to adjust parameters or escalate for review. The model is trained on your own historical deviation-to-failure correlation, not generic pharma data.

How long before we see measurable ROI?

The Learn stage is specifically designed to answer this. After 30 days of parallel running, we show you the delta: forecast error rate before vs. after, batch deviations flagged early vs. missed, territory alerts triggered in time vs. post-quarter discovery. Typical demand forecasting pilots show 18–30% reduction in forecast error within the first 30 days on high-velocity SKUs. We do not project ROI — we measure it on your data.
