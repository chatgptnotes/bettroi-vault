# Predictive Analytics for Logistics Companies

## Stop Running Your Fleet on Yesterday's Data

Bettroi builds predictive analytics systems for logistics companies that flag demand surges, route failures, and maintenance needs before they cost you revenue — not after.

---

## The Problem

Mid-to-large logistics operators in India are still making dispatch, fleet, and inventory decisions based on weekly reports and gut instinct. Demand spikes get missed by 2–3 days, vehicles break down mid-route because PM schedules are calendar-based instead of condition-based, and warehouse fill rates swing unpredictably because nobody has a reliable 14-day forward view of inbound volume. The result: SLA breaches, emergency vendor costs, and drivers sitting idle while freight piles up.

## What We Build for You

- **Demand forecasting agent** — predicts shipment volume by lane, zone, or SKU category 7–21 days out, trained on your TMS + historical data
- **Predictive maintenance alert system** — monitors vehicle telematics (mileage, engine hours, fault codes) and fires maintenance tickets before breakdowns occur
- **Route risk scorer** — flags lanes with elevated delay probability based on weather, traffic patterns, and historical carrier performance

## How We Do It: The Be AI-First Framework

We don't drop a model and disappear. Every engagement follows five stages:

**1. Discovery (Week 1–2)**
We audit your current data flows — TMS, WMS, GPS telematics, ERP — and identify the 3 prediction problems with the highest measurable ROI. You get a written findings report with baseline metrics.

**2. Learn (Weeks 3–8)**
We pilot one agent — typically the demand forecasting or predictive maintenance agent — against your live data for 30 days. We measure baseline error rates vs. AI-assisted prediction accuracy side by side.

**3. Wire (Weeks 6–10)**
Winning agents get integrated into your existing systems — TMS, fleet management platforms, WhatsApp ops groups, or internal dashboards — via API or lightweight connectors.

**4. Automate (Weeks 8–12)**
Manual escalation handoffs, morning status calls, and end-of-day exception reviews get replaced with agent-driven orchestration. Your ops team stops pulling reports and starts acting on alerts.

**5. Scale**
Agents roll out across additional lanes, depots, or business units with monitoring dashboards and governance protocols so your team owns the system, not just uses it.

## Frequently asked questions

**Our TMS and fleet data live in three different systems. Can you still build this?**

Yes — and this is the norm, not the exception. During the 2-week Discovery audit we map every data source you have: TMS exports, GPS telematics APIs, WMS feeds, even manual Excel trackers. We build lightweight connectors or ETL pipelines to unify the data before any model touches it. You don't need a clean data warehouse to start.

**How accurate are the demand forecasts, realistically?**

On structured historical data with 12+ months of shipment records, lane-level demand forecasts typically hit 80–88% accuracy at a 7-day horizon. That's enough to reduce emergency vehicle allocation by 20–30% in most operations. We measure baseline vs. AI accuracy in the Learn stage and show you the delta before you commit to full deployment.

**We already have a BI dashboard with some charts. How is this different?**

BI dashboards show you what happened. Predictive analytics agents tell you what's about to happen and, in the Automate stage, act on it without waiting for a human to read the chart. The practical difference: your ops manager gets a WhatsApp alert that Carrier X has a 73% delay probability on the Pune-Nagpur lane tomorrow, not a Monday morning report showing last week's SLA breach.

**What's the minimum fleet or shipment volume where this makes sense?**

Predictive models need enough data to find patterns. As a rule of thumb: 500+ shipments per month on tracked lanes, or a fleet of 30+ vehicles with telematics data. Below that, we'd likely recommend a simpler rules-based alerting system in Discovery rather than a full ML model — and we'll tell you that honestly in Week 2.

**How long before we see ROI?**

Most clients see measurable impact during the Learn stage itself — typically by Week 6. Predictive maintenance alone tends to pay back the pilot fee within one prevented breakdown. Full ROI calculations (fuel savings, SLA penalty avoidance, labour reallocation) are documented in the Discovery report so you have numbers to take to your management before committing to the full transformation.
